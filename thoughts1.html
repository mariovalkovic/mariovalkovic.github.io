Why did I decide to change my career path?

  • 28 Nov 2022
  • Mario Valkovic

At the beginning of high school I thought I would study programming, but then something changed and instead of informatics (in addition to math) I took the final exam from physics and enrolled in architecture. Little did I know what that was going to be about. I never really liked drawing, but geometry was my favorite subject and I loved modeling things with the 3D software SketchUp.

At first, I enjoyed architecture and there were so many new things to learn. But once you know all the software that is used in the industry, you get to a point where the only thing you can improve on is your knowledge of building processes and materials. I have always been more interested in the design process and the use of optimal tools than in the final design of a building itself.

During my studies, I was constantly learning new software. Often I chose to complete assignments with slightly lower quality in order to work on them in a new program. Another thing I always strived for was data reusability. Vector graphics instead of raster graphics, Revit instead of SketchUp, Grasshopper script to iterate the design instead of manual modeling, etc.

Over the past few years, both studying and working, I have not felt intellectually challenged. I feel that I am not reaching my full potential in this slow industry that largely refuses to use modern tools.

Studying programming has shown me that I am addicted to learning new technologies, and the multitude of possibilities in computer programming excites me. I can fully utilize my analytical and problem-solving personality. I enjoy those moments when I get lost in something for hours, but I know the solution exists somewhere - you just have to find it (probably on the Internet).


"Fun" fact: In 2012 and 2013 I was using Ubuntu as my primary and only OS.
